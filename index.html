HOW IT ALL BEGAN

The curse of Hawkins

In the small town of Hawkins, Indiana, the mysterious disappearance of a child triggers a series of supernatural events that will change the lives of its inhabitants. As friends, family and authorities try to find him, they discover the existence of unknown and dangerous forces coming from a parallel dimension called "The Other Side" or "Upside Down". While the town faces terrifying creatures and hidden secrets from a government laboratory, a group of young friends joins forces to fight the inexplicable. On the way, they forge bonds of friendship and courage that force them to mature before their time, facing fears that go beyond what is imaginable.

THE REFLECTION OF AN ERA

Life in the 80's

Stranger Things is set in the 1980s, a period marked by political tensions and technological advances that defined everyday life in the United States. The Cold War was at its peak, generating distrust towards the government and an atmosphere of secrets and conspiracies. Music, cinema and popular culture of the time reflected a spirit of adventure and curiosity, while technology began to change how people interact and inform themselves.

Hawkins seems like a typical American town, but beneath its peaceful appearance hide secret scientific experiments and inexplicable phenomena that reflect the concerns and fears of the era, making the 1980s a perfect setting for mixing mystery, science and the supernatural that characterizes the series.

MORE THAN FICTION

The themes in Stranger Things

The series combines the supernatural with the human, exploring friendship and loyalty of a group of young people who face unimaginable dangers together, as well as the strength of family bonds against external threats. The series reflects fear and courage when facing the unknown, curiosity to discover hidden secrets and conspiracies surrounding dangerous government experiments.

Through these elements, the story also addresses personal growth, sacrifice and the importance of staying united in adversity, creating an intense narrative full of mystery and emotion that connects with the viewer.
